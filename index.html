What is Financial Freedom?

Financial freedom means having enough savings, investments, and passive income to cover your living expenses for the rest of your life without being dependent on a salary. Our free financial freedom calculator helps you find your exact "freedom number" — the total nest egg you need.

The 4% Rule Explained

The 4% rule is a popular retirement planning guideline. It states that if you withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually, your money should last 30+ years. So if you need $2,000/month ($24,000/year), you need a nest egg of $600,000. Our calculator uses this principle to calculate your personalized financial freedom number.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need for financial freedom?

You need a nest egg equal to your annual expenses divided by your investment return rate. If you want $2,000/month at 10% returns, you need $240,000. Use our free calculator above for your personalized number.

What is the 4% rule?

The 4% rule states you can safely withdraw 4% of your savings annually without running out of money for 30+ years. To generate $40,000/year, you need $1,000,000 saved.

How to calculate Zakat on savings?

Zakat is 2.5% of your net zakatable assets (cash, gold, investments minus debts) held for one full lunar year, provided they exceed the Nisab threshold. Use our free Zakat calculator above.

What is compound interest?

Compound interest means earning returns on both your principal AND previously earned interest. $10,000 at 10% annually becomes $25,937 in 10 years — without adding any more money!

How to calculate loan EMI?

EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1), where P is principal, r is monthly rate, and n is number of months. Our free Loan EMI Calculator does this instantly with a full amortization schedule.
